Position Sticky Practical Examples

Scroll down and watch how sticky elements stay visible until their parent section ends.

What is position: sticky?

Sticky behaves like relative at first, but when you scroll to a certain point, it sticks in place like fixed. It only sticks inside its own parent container.

HTML Basics

Sticky section headers are often used in blogs, docs, article pages, and long category lists.

When you scroll this section, the title stays at the top until the next section pushes it away.

That makes it easier for users to know which section they are currently reading.

Unlike fixed, sticky stays inside its parent area, so it feels more natural in many layouts.

Things To Remember

Sticky needs a value like top: 0 or top: 20px.

It also depends on the parent container. If a parent has certain overflow settings, sticky may not work as expected.

That is why sticky is best understood as: relative first, then fixed-like while scrolling, but only inside its parent.
